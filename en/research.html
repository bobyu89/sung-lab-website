Research

Four focus areas — cognitive training, swallowing rehabilitation, NP development, and innovative teaching — driving care quality through evidence-based research. See the Projects page for the grant list.

Research Focus

OUR FOCUS

① Cognitive Training & Dementia Care

We build multi-domain cognitive-training modules and examine their effects on working memory, attention, executive function, and overall cognition in older adults, extending to randomized controlled trials in mild cognitive impairment and mild dementia. Related meta-analyses compare emotion-oriented therapies for behavioral and cognitive outcomes in dementia, and multisensory stimulation for neuropsychiatric symptoms and cognitive function.

② Swallowing Health & Geriatric Rehabilitation

Double-blind randomized controlled trials test tongue-resistance and strengthening training for tongue strength and oropharyngeal swallowing in frail older adults, extending to synergistic tongue-and-chin resistance training for swallowing, oral intake, and cognition in community-dwelling frail elders. Related work includes network meta-analyses of swallowing rehabilitation therapies for adult dysphagia, studies of laryngeal penetration and aspiration, and Iowa Oral Performance Instrument assessment of tongue strength, endurance, and pressure as predictors of dysphagia.

③ NP Development & Nursing Administration

The "Hearing Nightingale's Voice" project explored relationships among NP practice environment, leadership style, and job satisfaction, extended into a Chinese-journal study of organizational climate and leadership. Meta-analyses examine the clinical effectiveness and cost-effectiveness of primary community health care nurses and the global prevalence and moderators of nurse turnover, informing workforce policy. From 2026, an MOE Teaching Practice Research grant develops a precision clinical-reasoning teaching model using adaptive learning and multi-layered scaffolding within the "NP Role and Development" course.

④ Innovative Teaching & 3D Printing

We develop 3D-printed implantable central venous catheter models for training courses, studying their impact on nurses' skills and patient discharge education, and innovate e-cigarette prevention education through cluster randomized trials of health lectures using low-cost 3D-printed models that visualize vaping-associated lung injury. We also design educational board games to evaluate effects on clinicians' empathy and emotional intelligence — renewing clinical training through gamified, tangible teaching tools.
